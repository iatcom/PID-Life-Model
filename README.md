# 🧠 Human Response Simulator (PID Control)

A modern, interactive web-based simulator that maps the principles of **Control Theory (PID)** to human performance and psychology. This tool visualizes how the balance of raw effort, past experience, and strategic planning dictates how we respond to life's targets.

## 🛠 The Analogy: PID as Human Behavior

This simulator rebrands the standard $K_p$, $K_i$, and $K_d$ parameters to help users understand how different traits affect their "path to a goal."

### 1. Hard Work ($K_p$ - Proportional)
*   **The Drive:** This is your immediate reaction force. High hard work gets you moving fast but leads to **Burnout** if not tempered. It closes the gap between where you are and where you want to be.

### 2. Experience ($Ki$ - Integral)
*   **The Consistency:** This is the memory of past gaps. It ensures that you don't just get *close* to your goal, but that you actually finish the job. However, too much "Experience" (dwelling on the past) can cause slow, heavy oscillations in your progress.

### 3. Planning ($Kd$ - Derivative)
*   **The Strategic Brake:** Planning looks at your current trajectory and predicts the future. It acts as the "dampener" that prevents you from crashing into the finish line or overshooting your capacity. It is the essential cure for **Burnout**.

---

## 📊 Key Metrics Tracked

*   **Initiative:** How quickly you react to a new target (Rise Time).
*   **Burnout:** The percentage by which you over-exerted and passed your target (Overshoot).
*   **Stability Time:** How long it takes to settle into a sustainable rhythm (Settling Time).
*   **Reliability:** Your final standing relative to the goal (Steady State).
